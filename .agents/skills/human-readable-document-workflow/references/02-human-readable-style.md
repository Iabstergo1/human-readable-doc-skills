# Human Readable Style

Human-readable writing is specific, clear, contextual, and paced naturally.

## Principles

- Prefer concrete nouns and verbs over broad abstractions.
- Explain context before expecting the reader to accept conclusions.
- Use professional terms when needed, but define them on first use.
- Vary sentence length naturally without becoming theatrical.
- Preserve accuracy; do not trade correctness for casualness.
- Make transitions reflect real logic, not template rhythm.
- End with useful closure, not generic inspiration.

## Practical Checks

- Can a reader tell what changed, why it matters, and what to do next?
- Are claims tied to source material, examples, or clear assumptions?
- Are headings informative without sounding like marketing copy?
- Are lists used because comparison or scanning helps?
- Does each paragraph move the document forward?
